How to read a boring receipt

The repetition is the interface.

HomeReceiptsHow to readNo-deltaArchiveAboutContactMarkdown ↗
A receipt is not a leaderboard entry. It is a small public proof that a specific command shape ran on a specific machine under a specific state, and that its limits are visible enough to contest.

The fixed reading order

  1. Claim - the smallest thing the receipt says happened.
  2. Target - engine, regime, repo, branch, commit, build, model, dataset if any.
  3. Command - the exact command shape or command chain.
  4. Environment - OS, driver, CUDA, GPU, VRAM, resident processes, endpoint.
  5. Results - metrics with coordinates, never a single speed score.
  6. Quality gate - the invariant that should not move.
  7. Evidence - paths, logs, stdout excerpts, hashes when available.
  8. What this receipt does not prove - the boundary of the claim.
  9. Next step - the smallest action that would sharpen the receipt.

Six rules

  • A receipt re-executes.
  • Coordinates beat scores.
  • Negative, blocked and no-delta results have equal standing.
  • The quality gate is not an axis.
  • The card is not the body: RealRAG/EPKV live in the sibling research repo.
  • Reproducibility is not neutrality.

Status vocabulary

PASScommand completed and stated quality gate passed
BLOCKEDthe stack resisted; the blocker is the finding
MIXEDpart of the gate passed and part failed
NO DELTAthe intervention did not materially change the baseline
NOT EXERCISEDno behavioral gate was run; useful only when declared
