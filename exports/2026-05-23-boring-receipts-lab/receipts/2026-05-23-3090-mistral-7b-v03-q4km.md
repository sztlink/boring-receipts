## Reading

**Decode tracks parameter count, cleanly:** Mistral (7.25B) is fastest at 149 t/s,
Llama (8.03B) slowest at 140, Qwen (7.62B) in between - exactly the size order.
VRAM does the same. Decode is memory-bandwidth-bound, so fewer params = fewer bytes
moved per token = faster. Nothing surprising - and the un-surprising, specific
number is the product.

**Prefill does *not* track size as cleanly:** Qwen (7.62B) prefills fastest (5383)
despite being larger than Mistral (5054). Prefill is compute-bound and shaped by
architecture (attention/FFN dims, GQA group size), not just parameter count. So
"smaller = faster" holds for decode but not strictly for prefill - a small caveat
worth having a number for.
