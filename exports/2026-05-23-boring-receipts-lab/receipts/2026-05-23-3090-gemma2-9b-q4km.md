## Reading

Gemma-2-9B sits clearly below the 7–8B band: decode 102.8 t/s (vs 140–149) and VRAM
6.9 GiB (vs 4.9–5.6). Decode tracks parameter count, as established - bigger model,
fewer tokens per second.

But note the **drop is steeper than parameters alone**: Gemma is +15% params over
Llama-3.1-8B yet decode falls −26% (140 → 103). Gemma-2's architecture is heavier
than its param count suggests (wider FFN, attention logit soft-capping, alternating
local/global attention). So "decode ∝ params" is a good first-order rule *within*
an architecture, but across architectures the constant shifts - a caveat the
specific numbers make visible. Prefill confirms it too: Gemma's 4052 is the lowest
in the set despite not being the largest jump in params.
