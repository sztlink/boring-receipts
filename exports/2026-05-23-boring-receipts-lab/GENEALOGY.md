# Genealogy

Boring Receipts Lab has a genealogy because proof is never born neutral. Naming the
path prevents the lab from becoming a decontextualized benchmark shelf.

## Origin

The working phrase emerged during the AYA Crypto Infra Lab exploration on
2026-05-22. The useful part of that detour was not crypto income, tokenization or
financial speculation. It was the recognition that Felipe/AYA could be a sober
NVIDIA validation node for the Waffle/TurboQuant field.

The energy of the detour was redirected:

```txt
crypto speculation -> hardware validation -> boring receipts
```

That redirection is part of the work. A speculative surface was metabolized into a
public proof apparatus.

## Field of interlocution

The immediate field is Waffle/TurboQuant:

- llama.cpp / long-context / KV-cache pressure;
- TurboQuant and related KV dtype/compression questions;
- TriAttention / longctx / MTP branches when they need independent NVIDIA receipts;
- RealRAG/EPKV as research siblings, not canonical runtime axes.

## Division of roles

```txt
Boring Receipts Lab       -> public reproducibility card
sztlink/turboquant-cuda-bench -> research/probe body
Waffle/TurboQuant field   -> interlocution and branch requests
AYA-3090 / AYA-4090       -> material validation nodes
```

The card must remain portable. The research body may remain dense.

## Why the name matters

"Boring" is not modesty. It is a method:

- no vague benchmark claims;
- no single speed score;
- no leaderboard posture;
- no hidden machine state;
- no erasure of blocked/no-delta runs.

The receipt is boring so the claim can be audited.

## Historical boundary

Do not let the genealogy disappear from the public surface. If the lab scales, keep
visible that it began as a refusal of hype: a detour away from speculative rhetoric
and toward reproducible, preservable technical evidence.
